# Fake-Job-Posting-Analysis
Fake Job Posting Analysis is a comprehensive data analytics project that investigates patterns and indicators associated with fraudulent job listings on online recruitment platforms. With the rapid growth of digital job boards, scammers increasingly exploit job seekers by posting misleading or fake job advertisements. This project aims to analyze job posting data to identify characteristics that help distinguish legitimate job opportunities from fraudulent ones.

The analysis is performed using a dataset containing thousands of job postings labeled as real or fake. Using Python for data processing and visualization, along with SQL for structured data analysis, the project explores multiple attributes of job listings such as employment type, salary information, company profile availability, requirements, benefits, and job description content. Through feature engineering, additional indicators such as text length, number of missing fields, presence of free email domains, and telecommuting flags are extracted to better understand the structure of suspicious postings.

The project also includes keyword-based scam detection, where commonly used fraudulent phrases such as “work from home,” “earn money,” “no experience required,” and similar promotional language are analyzed to determine how frequently they appear in fake job listings compared to legitimate ones. These linguistic patterns provide insight into how scammers craft deceptive job descriptions.

To present findings clearly, the project generates multiple visualizations and an analytical dashboard highlighting trends such as fraud rate by employment type, geographic distribution of fake postings, impact of missing information fields, email domain patterns, behavioral indicators, and text length differences between real and fake job listings.

The results reveal several important red flags, including postings with missing company information, use of free personal email domains, remote job offers without salary details, and excessive use of promotional keywords. These insights can help job platforms, recruiters, and job seekers identify suspicious listings more effectively and improve fraud detection mechanisms.

This project demonstrates practical applications of data analysis, exploratory data analysis (EDA), SQL querying, feature engineering, and data visualization in solving real-world problems related to online job market safety.
